DATA PROCESSING AGREEMENT

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") is entered into as of January 15, 2024 and forms part of the Master Services Agreement dated December 1, 2023 (the "Agreement") between:

DataTech Solutions Inc., a Delaware corporation with principal offices at 789 Cloud Street, Seattle, WA 98101 ("Data Processor" or "Processor")

AND

Enterprise Healthcare Systems Ltd., a UK private limited company with principal offices at 321 Medical Park, Manchester M1 1AA ("Data Controller" or "Controller")

RECITALS

WHEREAS, under the Agreement, Processor provides cloud-based data analytics and storage services to Controller;

WHEREAS, in the course of providing such services, Processor will Process Personal Data on behalf of Controller;

WHEREAS, the Parties wish to comply with applicable Data Protection Laws, including the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR") and the UK Data Protection Act 2018;

NOW, THEREFORE, the Parties agree as follows:

1. DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION

1.1 Unless otherwise defined herein, capitalized terms shall have the meanings set forth in the GDPR:

"Data Protection Laws" means all applicable laws and regulations relating to the Processing of Personal Data, including the GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA, and any successor legislation.

"Data Subject" means an identified or identifiable natural person about whom Personal Data relates.

"Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person that is Processed by Processor on behalf of Controller under this DPA.

"Personal Data Breach" means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data.

"Processing" (and "Process") means any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, including collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, dissemination, erasure, or destruction.

"Sub-processor" means any third party engaged by Processor to Process Personal Data on behalf of Controller.

"Supervisory Authority" means an independent public authority established by an EU Member State pursuant to the GDPR.

2. SCOPE AND DETAILS OF PROCESSING

2.1 Nature and Purpose of Processing: Processor shall Process Personal Data solely for the purpose of providing the cloud-based analytics and storage services as described in the Agreement and as further instructed by Controller in writing.

2.2 Duration: Processor shall Process Personal Data for the duration of the Agreement, unless otherwise specified in writing.

2.3 Types of Personal Data: The Personal Data processed under this DPA includes:
- Patient identifiers (names, dates of birth, medical record numbers)
- Contact information (addresses, phone numbers, email addresses)
- Health information (diagnoses, treatment records, prescriptions, lab results)
- Insurance information (policy numbers, coverage details, claims data)
- Billing and payment information
- Demographic information (age, gender, ethnicity)

2.4 Categories of Data Subjects: The Data Subjects whose Personal Data is processed include:
- Current and former patients
- Healthcare providers and medical staff
- Insurance beneficiaries
- Emergency contacts and legal representatives

3. OBLIGATIONS OF THE PROCESSOR

3.1 Instructions: Processor shall Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Controller, unless required to do so by EU or Member State law. If Processor believes an instruction infringes Data Protection Laws, it shall immediately inform Controller.

3.2 Confidentiality:
(a) Processor shall ensure that persons authorized to Process Personal Data have committed themselves to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality.
(b) Processor shall maintain the confidentiality of Personal Data and shall not disclose it to third parties without Controller's prior written authorization, except as required by law.

3.3 Security Measures:
(a) Processor shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including:
    i. Pseudonymization and encryption of Personal Data;
    ii. Ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience of Processing systems;
    iii. Regular testing, assessment, and evaluation of effectiveness of security measures;
    iv. Multi-factor authentication for system access;
    v. Regular security training for personnel;
    vi. Intrusion detection and prevention systems;
    vii. Data loss prevention (DLP) solutions;
    viii. Regular vulnerability assessments and penetration testing.

(b) Processor shall maintain ISO 27001 and SOC 2 Type II certifications throughout the term of this DPA.

(c) Processor shall implement and maintain:
    - End-to-end encryption in transit (TLS 1.3 or higher)
    - Encryption at rest (AES-256 or equivalent)
    - Secure key management practices
    - Network segmentation and access controls
    - Comprehensive logging and monitoring
    - Incident response procedures

3.4 Sub-processing:
(a) Controller provides general authorization for Processor to engage Sub-processors, subject to the conditions set forth in this Section.

(b) Processor shall maintain a current list of Sub-processors (Annex B) and notify Controller at least thirty (30) days in advance of any intended changes concerning the addition or replacement of Sub-processors.

(c) Controller may object to the appointment of a new Sub-processor on reasonable grounds within fifteen (15) days of notification. If Controller objects, the Parties shall work together in good faith to find a mutually acceptable resolution.

(d) Processor shall impose data protection obligations on Sub-processors that are substantially the same as those set forth in this DPA, including requirements regarding security, confidentiality, and data transfers.

(e) Processor shall remain fully liable to Controller for the performance of Sub-processors' obligations.

3.5 Data Subject Rights:
(a) Taking into account the nature of Processing, Processor shall assist Controller by implementing appropriate technical and organizational measures to fulfill Controller's obligation to respond to requests from Data Subjects exercising their rights under Data Protection Laws, including:
    - Right of access (Article 15 GDPR)
    - Right to rectification (Article 16 GDPR)
    - Right to erasure (Article 17 GDPR)
    - Right to restriction of processing (Article 18 GDPR)
    - Right to data portability (Article 20 GDPR)
    - Right to object (Article 21 GDPR)

(b) Processor shall notify Controller without undue delay if it receives a request from a Data Subjec exercising any of the above rights.

(c) Processor shall not respond to such requests except on documented instructions from Controller or as required by applicable law.

3.6 Personal Data Breach Notification:
(a) Processor shall notify Controller without undue delay and in any event within twenty-four (24) hours of becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach.

(b) Such notification shall include, at a minimum:
    i. Description of the nature of the Personal Data Breach;
    ii. Categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and Personal Data records affected;
    iii. Name and contact details of the data protection officer or other contact point;
    iv. Description of likely consequences of the Personal Data Breach;
    v. Description of measures taken or proposed to address the breach and mitigate adverse effects;
    vi. Timeline of the breach discovery and containment.

(c) Processor shall provide reasonable assistance to Controller in notifying Supervisory Authorities and Data Subjects as required by Data Protection Laws.

(d) Processor shall document all Personal Data Breaches and make such documentation available to Controller and Supervisory Authorities upon request.

3.7 Data Protection Impact Assessment and Prior Consultation:
(a) Processor shall provide reasonable assistance to Controller in ensuring compliance with obligations under Articles 35 and 36 GDPR regarding data protection impact assessments and prior consultation with Supervisory Authorities.

(b) Processor shall provide Controller with information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with obligations under this DPA and Data Protection Laws.

3.8 Deletion or Return of Personal Data:
(a) Upon termination or expiration of the Agreement, Processor shall, at Controller's choice and within thirty (30) days:
    i. Delete all Personal Data and existing copies (unless EU or Member State law requires storage); or
    ii. Return all Personal Data to Controller in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format.

(b) Processor shall provide written certification of deletion or return within fifteen (15) days of completing such actions.

(c) Processor may retain Personal Data to the extent required by applicable law, subject to the continuing confidentiality obligations of this DPA.

4. AUDITS AND COMPLIANCE

4.1 Processor shall make available to Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA and allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by Controller or an auditor mandated by Controller.

4.2 Controller may conduct audits no more than once per year, upon thirty (30) days' prior written notice, during normal business hours and in a manner that does not unreasonably interfere with Processor's business operations.

4.3 Processor shall maintain records of Processing activities under its responsibility as required by Article 30(2) GDPR and make such records available to Controller and Supervisory Authorities upon request.

5. INTERNATIONAL DATA TRANSFERS

5.1 Processor may transfer Personal Data outside the European Economic Area only if:
(a) The transfer is to a country or territory ensuring an adequate level of protection as determined by the European Commission; or
(b) Appropriate safeguards are in place, including Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission; or
(c) The transfer is necessary for one of the reasons specified in Article 49 GDPR.

5.2 The Parties agree to enter into Standard Contractual Clauses as set forth in Annex C to this DPA for any transfers of Personal Data to third countries not subject to an adequacy decision.

5.3 Processor shall implement supplementary measures where necessary to ensure an adequate level of protection for transferred Personal Data, taking into account applicable guidance from Supervisory Authorities and the European Data Protection Board.

6. CONTROLLER OBLIGATIONS

6.1 Controller represents and warrants that:
(a) It has complied and will comply with all applicable Data Protection Laws regarding the collection and disclosure of Personal Data to Processor;
(b) It has provided or will provide all required notices to Data Subjects and obtained all necessary consents for Processing;
(c) The Processing instructions provided to Processor comply with Data Protection Laws.

6.2 Controller shall be responsible for responding to all requests from Data Subjects and complaints regarding Processing, except to the extent Processor is required to assist under Section 3.5.

7. LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

7.1 Each Party's liability arising out of or related to this DPA shall be subject to the limitations and exclusions of liability set forth in the Agreement.

7.2 Processor shall indemnify and hold harmless Controller from any fines, penalties, costs, or damages incurred as a result of Processor's breach of this DPA or Data Protection Laws.

7.3 The limitations of liability in the Agreement shall not apply to:
(a) Breaches of confidentiality obligations;
(b) Violations of Data Protection Laws resulting from Processor's wilful misconduct or gross negligence;
(c) Personal Data Breaches caused by Processor's failure to implement required security measures.

8. TERM AND TERMINATION

8.1 This DPA shall commence on the Effective Date and continue in effect for so long as Processor Processes Personal Data on behalf of Controller under the Agreement.

8.2 Controller may terminate this DPA immediately upon written notice if Processor materially breaches any provision and fails to remedy such breach within thirty (30) days of receiving notice.

8.3 Upon termination, the provisions regarding deletion or return of Personal Data (Section 3.8) shall apply.

9. GENERAL PROVISIONS

9.1 Governing Law: This DPA shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws governing the Agreement.

9.2 Conflict: In the event of any conflict between this DPA and the Agreement, this DPA shall prevail to the extent of such conflict.

9.3 Amendment: This DPA may be amended only by written agreement of both Parties, except that Processor may amend this DPA as necessary to comply with changes in Data Protection Laws upon thirty (30) days' notice to Controller.

9.4 Severability: If any provision of this DPA is held invalid or unenforceable, that provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid and enforceable, and the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

9.5 Order of Precedence: In case of conflict, the order of precedence shall be: (1) Standard Contractual Clauses, (2) this DPA, (3) the Agreement.
